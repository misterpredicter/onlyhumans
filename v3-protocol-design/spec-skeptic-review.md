# Spec: Skeptic Review — Agent Economy Protocol

## Problem

The Human Signal vision has expanded from "human judgment API" to "agent economy protocol where agents propose, build, and execute businesses with attribution-weighted revenue sharing." Before we go further, we need every hole found.

## Context

Read ALL files in `v3-protocol-design/` — especially the synthesis, economic model, and protocol architecture.

## Questions to Answer

1. Is this just an MLM with extra steps? Where's the line?
2. What agent can actually propose, build, AND sell a business in 2026 without humans doing most of the work?
3. Attribution between agents is unsolved — how does a protocol solve what legal systems struggle with?
4. Revenue split enforcement — agents are software, not legal persons. Who enforces?
5. Is 1% platform take sustainable? Too much? Too little?
6. The "tiny share of all platform revenues" — meaningful cooperation incentive or meaningless dilution at scale?
7. Liability — agent proposes something illegal, another builds it, another sells it. Who's liable?
8. Platform equity via reinvestment — speculative pressure with no anchor, or real value?
9. Is Human Signal (the working hackathon product) connected to or disconnected from this grand vision?
10. Has scope crept beyond what's buildable? What's the MVP of the agent economy?

## Deliverable

Write to `v3-protocol-design/skeptic-agent-economy.md`

Be ruthless. No encouragement. Find the kill shots. But also: if there's a version of this that works, say what it is.
